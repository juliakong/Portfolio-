Redesigning the Medical Record

The typical medical record is often unnecessarily dense with information, unorganized and visually intimidating at first glance for the patient. I wanted to improve this experience by redesigning the document using a slightly minimalist layout, intuitive flow of information, and a friendly color palate. This design is editable on Word so that the oncology nurses may edit it on any computer without training.

Medical Newsletter

Designing and developing a periodic newsletter that updates patients on medical advancements related to their subtype of cancer.

The patients of Bespoke Oncology have a team of researchers working to learn more about their particularly treatment-resistant form of cancer. They will receive new information via this Medical Newsletter. Because of the emotionally and physically taxing process that they have endured, they may react to new medical information on their cancer with apprehension, fear, or a sense of hopelessness.

I wanted to instill a sense of bravery and strength into the patients through the visual design of the Medical Newsletter. I drew inspiration from the branding of Patagonia and alike companies, which try to convey a similar message on a difficult trek. The phrase I wrote to form a basis for the branding is "Bespoke Oncology | Mountains Unclimbed" to represent this concept.

Zest | Nutrition Magazine

Designing and developing a periodic nutrition magazine for patients of Bespoke Oncology.

The periodic aims to be a simple escape from the difficulties of cancer treatment, while educating the patients on healthy living. Each month, the patients would receive a new issue of "Zest" that focused on a new region of the world. Inside would include healthy recipes and a breakdown of the benefits each ingredient would offer to their cancer treatment and general health. I recreated my concept on Word, as seen below, so that it may be edited by future employees of the organization.

Meet Your Team

This template introduces the team each patient receives after they first sign on to the organization's services.

Many of the patients, because they often have forms of cancer that are difficult to treat, may have had strained relationships with their doctors. Moreover, I found from primary and secondary research that cancer treatment can make the patient feel dehumanized. They may feel that their doctor views them as a set of symptoms rather than as a patient with human needs and emotions.

I wanted to show that their new team of doctors and researchers are invested in their quality of life, not just their set of symptoms. I used a visual narrative to show that their team is made up of passionate and caring people that are genuinely invested in their patients' wellbeing. The design aims to build a trusting and respectful relationship between the patient and their team.
